Covid-19

Early Covid-19

At the beginning of Covid I like many others had the idea that it would not really affect me or my life in a significant way and would be treated like the common flu but I was also like many wrong. It was a big shift in my education to be told that I would be suddenly be online for my classes using Zoom which was a program I was unfarmiliar with.

Mid Covid-19

Being unable to go out to see my friends was difficult for me as I enjoy being able to go out and have fun with a few friends on the weekends since we all lived in different parts of Kilkenny making it difficult to meet up.

So with the Covid restrictions prohibiting us from seeing each other in person was a hard thing to grow accustomed to. So playing videogames and chatting online was the only way we could keep in touch with each other.

Late Covid-19

Doing my Leaving Certificate during a global pandemic was honestly a lot scarier to me than the pandemic itself due to the amount of uncertainty I as a student was left with.

The fact that something like this had never happened before left me with a lot of questions that nobody could answer which was very stressful initially but the introduction of accredited grades was a big help and allowed me to get the college course I wanted.

Covid-19 Now

Even though most of the restrictions have been lifted and life is beginning to return to normal, Covid-19 still poses a big threat to us in the coming months.

The thought of a new variant of the virus causing another lockdown is something that is always in the back of my mind nowadays but I do have to be thankful that no one I know or I got sick with Covid during the pandemic so far.
